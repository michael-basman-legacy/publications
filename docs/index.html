Rabbits Review and Popular Chess

Rabbits Review and Popular Chess were magazines published in the UK between 1979 and 1987, aimed at amateur chess players graded below about 150 BCF.

The first 6 issues of Rabbits Review were edited by B.P. Floyd, with Michael Basman, Simon Webb and Tim Harding as regular contributors. From issue 7 onwards, Michael Basman took over editorship, and ran it until 1981 at which point the magazine was relaunched as Popular Chess.
